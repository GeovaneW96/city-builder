# Art Direction

## Visual Style

Recommended style:

> Cozy low-poly diorama city.

This style is appropriate because it is:

- readable from a distance;
- performant in browser;
- achievable with small asset scope;
- compatible with procedural/modular buildings;
- forgiving compared to realism.

## Visual Goals

The city should feel:

- charming;
- clear;
- playful;
- alive;
- readable.

It should not feel:

- photorealistic;
- visually noisy;
- asset-heavy;
- dark or gritty;
- overloaded with tiny details.

## Camera

Recommended camera:

- isometric or angled top-down;
- limited pitch;
- smooth zoom;
- optional rotation;
- strong visibility of grid and buildings.

## Building Style

Buildings should use:

- simple geometry;
- category-specific silhouettes;
- clear colors/materials;
- small animated details later.

Residential:

- warm;
- small roofs;
- cozy scale.

Commercial:

- storefronts;
- signs;
- brighter accents.

Industrial:

- larger footprints;
- smoke stacks;
- grey/brown tones;
- visible pollution later.

Services:

- recognizable silhouettes;
- clear icons;
- distinct colors.

## Environment

Initial environment:

- grass tiles;
- subtle grid;
- simple roads;
- simple sky/background;
- optional trees/decorations.

Later:

- terrain variation;
- water;
- forests;
- resource deposits;
- landmarks.

## Visual Feedback

Important visual states:

- valid placement;
- invalid placement;
- selected tile;
- hover tile;
- building under construction;
- abandoned building;
- no power/water warning;
- service coverage;
- pollution.

## Animation

Use simple animations:

- building pop-in;
- construction progress;
- UI milestone celebration;
- subtle car movement later.

Avoid excessive animation that hurts readability.

## Asset Pipeline

Early development can use:

- procedural boxes;
- placeholder low-poly meshes;
- simple generated models;
- asset ids in building data.

Do not block gameplay development on final art.
