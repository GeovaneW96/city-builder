# Game Vision

## Working Title

City Builder Game

The title is temporary. The project should not commit to branding until the first playable version has a clearer identity.

## One-Sentence Pitch

A cozy, browser-based 3D city builder where players grow a small settlement into a thriving city by balancing roads, zoning, services, budget, happiness, traffic, and expansion.

## Core Fantasy

The player is the mayor and planner of a living city.

They start with empty land and limited money. Through smart planning, they turn simple roads and empty zones into neighborhoods, shops, factories, parks, schools, services, and eventually a complex city.

The emotional fantasy is:

> "I built this city, and I understand why it works."

## Design Pillars

### 1. Clarity First

The player should understand what is happening and why.

Bad:

- buildings fail silently;
- stats change without explanation;
- warnings do not tell the player what to do.

Good:

- clear warning icons;
- readable overlays;
- simple cause/effect;
- explainable simulation.

### 2. Satisfying Growth

The city should visibly evolve.

Examples:

- empty land becomes neighborhoods;
- small homes become denser buildings;
- roads form networks;
- commercial streets emerge;
- service buildings improve nearby areas;
- districts develop different identities.

### 3. Meaningful Tradeoffs

Every major decision should have benefits and costs.

Examples:

- industry creates jobs but causes pollution;
- high taxes create money but reduce happiness;
- parks improve happiness but cost upkeep;
- dense housing increases population but stresses services;
- roads reduce traffic but consume money and space.

### 4. Problems Are Gameplay

City problems are not interruptions. They are the game.

Examples:

- no power;
- lack of jobs;
- budget deficit;
- traffic congestion;
- pollution;
- low happiness;
- service gaps.

The player should be able to diagnose and solve these problems.

### 5. Browser-Native Accessibility

The game should be playable in a browser without requiring installation.

This implies:

- stylized visuals;
- strong performance discipline;
- modest map sizes at first;
- progressive complexity;
- good UI.

## Target Player

Primary target:

- likes city builders, management games, tycoon games, or strategy games;
- enjoys optimization and visible progression;
- wants a lighter, more accessible city builder than full-scale simulation games.

Secondary target:

- casual players who enjoy cozy building and progression.

## First Playable Identity

The first playable version should feel like:

- a small but satisfying city-builder scenario;
- easy to understand;
- hard enough to create interesting choices;
- visually charming;
- capable of failure through bankruptcy or poor planning.

It should not feel like:

- a full Cities: Skylines replacement;
- a realistic urban planning simulator;
- a decoration-only sandbox;
- a tech demo with no player goals.
